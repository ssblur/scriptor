# Tomes and Spellbooks

My first exposure to magic, and the reason I started writing these notes, were tomes.

Scattered around the world, in villages, mineshafts, and more, are ancient tomes that, when dusted off,
contain all sorts of spells!

<item href="scriptor:tome_tier1"></item>

These don't seem particularly rare, but the best-preserved, and often most powerful, seem to be much less common
than the cheaper, rattier books I've found scattered about.
I've even heard tale that some are guarded by powerful monsters, though I wouldn't dare to test my mettle 
against the likes of the Wither.

A couple of times, I've bumped into other Nominomists in villages, and while they don't seem to be as invested
in investigation as I am, they were willing to trade some of my rarer books for a couple of the ones that they
had copies of.

Spell tomes were also one of my first great successes! 
I'd tried casting spells I'd read [verbally](scriptor:casting/verbal_casting), but tomes let me synthesize 
entirely new spells from what I'd learned.

I've detailed pages on [writing tomes](scriptor:casting/writing_tomes) elsewhere.
